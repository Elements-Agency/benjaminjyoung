“Mistakes” is a raw and intimate watercolor portrait that lingers long after the first glance. The figure’s weary expression and hollow stillness carry the weight of a lifetime quietly replayed in memory — moments wished back, words never unsaid, and the invisible marks left by survival itself. Every translucent wash and muted shadow reveals the vulnerability of being human, while the restrained palette allows emotion to take center stage. Rather than dramatizing pain, the painting speaks in silence, inviting collectors into a deeply personal confrontation with regret, endurance, and self-reflection. It is a work that rewards prolonged viewing, revealing new emotional subtleties each time, making it not only a portrait, but a deeply human presence within a collection.

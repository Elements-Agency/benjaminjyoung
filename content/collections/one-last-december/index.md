A singular, pivotal chapter. This era represents the breaking point — the moment when the burden of loss felt unbearable. The work from this brief but powerful period carries a raw finality, confronting despair without disguise. It stands as a stark emotional landmark within the larger journey.

<!--more-->

Unlike the other eras, **One Last December** is not a long season of work—it is a moment. A brief but defining chapter that captures the point where emotional endurance finally reached its limit. After years of carrying the weight of loss and unresolved trauma, December of 2019 represents the breaking point where the artist could no longer continue living under the constant shadow of grief.

The paintings associated with this era carry an unusual stillness. They are not only expressions of sadness, but reflections of exhaustion—what happens when a person has spent years trying to survive emotional pain that never fully loosened its grip. In this period, the work becomes stark and honest, confronting the internal collapse that comes when someone can no longer pretend the burden is manageable.
